# House Overview

> Updated for v6.1 (2026-04-26) — Player Board format. All Houses use passive + special unit + house rule. **Once-per-game powers REMOVED in v6.0** (Decision 28). **Blood Rally and Healer's Art reworked to RT-recall in v6.1** (Decision 29).
> Base game: Mars, Minerva, Apollo, Diana. Expansion: Ceres, Jupiter.

---

## Quick Comparison (Base 4 Houses)

| House | Theme | Home Castle | Passive | Playstyle |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **Mars** | Aggression, Resilience | Hex 45 (Southern Reaches) | Red Rage: +1 outnumbered, AND wins ties when outnumbered | Rush, overwhelm, comeback |
| **Minerva** | Strategy, Intelligence | Hex 6 (Highlands) | Owl's Wisdom: See exact counts + readiness in adjacent hexes | Control, defense, economic |

## House Player Boards

### House Mars — The Wolves

| Ability | Name | Effect |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Passive** | Red Rage | +1 combat strength when Mars has fewer units than opponent in the hex |
| **Special Unit** | Howler | Counts as 2 Alert units for combat strength calculation |
| **House Rule** | Blood Rally (v6.1) | Win combat → recall 1 Mars unit from your Reserve Track to the combat hex (Alert, acted) |
| **Once Per Game** | Rage of Ares | Double all Mars combat strength for one battle |

**Wins by:** Aggressive combat, capturing flags, overwhelming enemies
**Signature Play:** Fight outnumbered with Red Rage bonus, win, Blood Rally a distant subdued ally. Momentum snowballs.
**Weakness:** No scouting advantages. Predictable aggression. Food pressure in Act III.

---

### House Minerva — The Owls

| Ability | Name | Effect |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Passive** | Owl's Wisdom | Can see exact unit counts and readiness states in all adjacent hexes (enhanced passive scouting) |
| **Special Unit** | Tactician | Scouts within 2 hexes as a free ability (no order consumed) |
| **House Rule** | Prepared Positions | When defending, Minerva's At Ease units fight as if Alert (+1 each instead of +0) |
| **Resource Bonus (v6.0)** | Highland Gatherers | Minerva Gatherers in Highland hexes produce 2 Food (instead of 1) |

**Wins by:** Defensive bunkers, information superiority, economic tempo
**Signature Play:** Tactician scouts 2 hexes ahead, Owl's Wisdom reveals an At Ease army. Highland Gatherers feed Minerva better than anyone else. Prepared Positions means attacking Minerva gatherers backfires.
**Weakness:** Lower raw offensive combat power. Castle assaults require committing forces away from defensive positions.

---

### House Apollo — The Suns

| Ability | Name | Effect |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Passive** | Sun's Reach | Free move range is 2 hexes instead of 1 |
| **Special Unit** | Archer | Grants +1 combat strength to any allied combat in an adjacent hex, without the Archer being in the fight |
| **House Rule** | Healer's Art (v6.1) | After any combat Apollo is in (win or lose), recall 1 Apollo unit from your Reserve Track to the combat hex |

**Wins by:** Mobility, precision strikes, disruption
**Signature Play:** 2-hex free move puts Apollo where no one expects. Archer provides ranged support from safety. Healer's Art means Apollo recovers from every fight.
**Weakness:** Isolated starting position (South Sea Coast). No combat-win card draw means Apollo needs castle captures or Discovery Sites to stockpile Tactics Cards.

---

### House Diana — The Shadows

| Ability | Name | Effect |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Passive** | The Hunt (v6.0) | When Diana wins ANY combat (attack or defense), surviving Diana units may move 1 hex |
| **Special Unit** | Shadow | Moves through enemy-occupied hexes without triggering combat |
| **House Rule** | Forest Hunters (v6.0) | Withdrawals from forest hexes hide the Removal Marker for 1 round |
| **Resource Bonus** | Up to 2 Diana units in Forest hexes are exempt from starvation upkeep |

**Wins by:** Stealth, ambush, sudden redeployment
**Signature Play:** Withdraw from a Greatwoods hex (Forest Hunters hides the marker), advance to Space 2-3 over a couple rounds, redeploy into a different Greatwoods hex (forest deployment bypasses passive scouting), strike. Then The Hunt advances survivors.
**Weakness:** No raw combat strength bonus. Lost the forest combat cover in v6.0 — gatherers in Greatwoods are now genuinely vulnerable, not bunker-tough.

## Matchup Notes

### Mars vs. Minerva
Aggression vs. information. Minerva's Owl's Wisdom sees Mars coming, and Prepared Positions makes Minerva hard to ambush. Mars counters by attacking on multiple fronts (Blood Rally recovers losses) and deploying from the Reserve Track where Minerva can't predict the destination. The race for Ceres Castle is their natural flashpoint.

### Mars vs. Diana
Brute force vs. stealth. Mars wants a decisive slugfest; Diana wants to ambush via forest deployment and strike where Mars's Alert sentries can't see. Mars's Red Rage tie-break is decisive in even fights, but Diana's Forest Hunters keeps her real position uncertain. Diana's gatherers in Greatwoods are no longer fortress-tough (no forest combat cover), so Diana must screen them with Alert defenders or accept the risk.

### Minerva vs. Apollo
Control vs. chaos. Minerva wants a predictable board; Apollo wants constant disruption. Apollo's 2-hex free move outmaneuvers Minerva's scouting perimeter, but Minerva's Prepared Positions means Apollo can't catch defenders off-guard.

### Diana vs. Apollo
Mobility vs. mobility. Apollo's 2-hex free move covers ground in any terrain; Diana's stealth (Forest Hunters + Shadow + universal forest-deployment bypass) makes her position hard to pin down. Apollo's Archer provides ranged support; Diana's Shadow can cross enemy territory unopposed. Apollo benefits from open terrain, Diana benefits from forest hexes — the map asymmetry decides which gets the better setup.

### Mars vs. Apollo
Raw power vs. speed. Mars wants a decisive battle; Apollo wants hit-and-run. Apollo's Healer's Art recovers from every skirmish, frustrating Mars's combat momentum. But if Mars catches Apollo committed, Red Rage + Howler overwhelms.

### Minerva vs. Diana
Information vs. concealment. Minerva sees adjacent hex compositions exactly (Owl's Wisdom) — but Diana's Forest Hunters hides her Removal Marker for a round, and forest deployments bypass Minerva's scouting perimeter. Minerva's Prepared Positions counter Diana's direct attacks (At Ease Minerva fights as Alert on defense), so Diana must use Shadow to bypass Minerva's front line and hit weaker hexes from behind.
