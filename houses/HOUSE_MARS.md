# House Mars — The Wolves

## Identity
**Theme:** Aggression, resilience, the will to rise
**Canon Reference:** House Mars, the warrior caste of the Institute
**Starting Zone:** Southern Reaches (Hex 52)
**Color:** Red

---

## Starting Setup
- Starting Hex: Mars Castle (Hex 45) and surrounding Southern Reaches
- Starting Resources: **7 Food** (v6.0)
- Units: 8 Regular, 1 Flag-bearer, 1 Howler (Special) — 5 in supply for Recruit

---

## Player Board

### Passive Ability: Red Rage
*"The more you bleed, the harder you fight."*

**+1 combat strength when outnumbered. AND Mars wins ties when outnumbered (v6.0).**

Mars is scariest when outnumbered. A 3 vs 5 fight is really 4 vs 5 for Mars — and if Mars's total ties, Mars wins instead of the defender (the Red Rage tie-break, added in v6.0 to fix a paradox where Red Rage's +1 only mattered when Mars was already losing). No activation cost, no once-per-game limit. Triggers automatically whenever Mars is the smaller force in unit count.

---

### Special Unit: The Howler
*"The Howler doesn't fight harder. The Howler fights like two."*

**Counts as 2 Alert units for combat strength.**

A hex with 3 Regular Alert units + 1 Howler = 5 combat strength (not 4). The Howler is the psychological weapon of Mars — its presence alone forces opponents to recalculate. If lost (subdued or converted), Mars loses significant combat punch.

---

### House Rule: Blood Rally (v6.1 rework)
*"We don't leave our own behind."*

**When Mars wins a combat, recall 1 Mars unit from your Reserve Track directly to the combat hex (Alert, acted). Free, no order required.**

Blood Rally triggers on every combat victory. Mars marches a previously-retreated soldier back into the fight at the front line. Combined with the v6.1 retreat-to-RT rule, this means Mars losses aren't permanent — every Mars unit pushed off the board is one Blood Rally away from being recalled to the next combat hex. Mars is the comeback house mechanically and thematically.

(In v6.0, Blood Rally rallied subdued units in any hex — but v6.1 retired the persistent subdued state. The recall-from-RT version is the closest functional equivalent and arguably more thematic: Mars doesn't just stand units back up; he marches reinforcements forward.)

---

## Once Per Game: REMOVED (v6.0)
Rage of Ares was removed in the v6.0 anti-turtle pass — Once Per Game powers had wildly uneven impact across Houses (Rage of Ares = +5-10 strength, Lunar Eclipse often did nothing). May be reintroduced if Mars feels weak in playtesting.

---

## Resource Bonus: REMOVED (v6.0)
The "win combat in Fertile hex = +1 Food" bonus was removed as redundant with Blood Rally — both were "winning rewards momentum" mechanics, and Mars was already the most combat-stacked House.

---

## Strategic Notes

Mars is the comeback king. Red Rage makes them dangerous when outnumbered. Blood Rally means subduing Mars without converting them just gives them rally fodder. The Howler provides raw combat power. Rage of Ares is the nuclear option.

**Early Game:** Mars should be fighting from Round 1. Red Rage rewards being the smaller force; Howler stacks +1 effective strength; Blood Rally keeps subdued allies recoverable.

**Mid Game:** Trade combat aggressively. Convert enemies (worth 2 VP each in v6.0) for army growth without paying Recruit costs. Push toward a neutral castle assault — capturing one is +5 VP, 2-4 troops, and 2 Tactics Cards.

**Late Game:** Push for flag capture or Domination. Mars with the Howler at the front of a Castle assault, supported by 5+ regulars, is the dominant late-game threat.

**Mars's Weakness:** No card-draw bonus. Mars can't earn Tactics Cards from combat anymore (v6.0) — for cards, Mars must capture castles or hit Discovery Sites. Pure aggression scores VP but leaves Mars cardless in the bluff layer. The compensation: Mars's combat math (Red Rage tie-break + Howler) often wins fights without needing cards.
