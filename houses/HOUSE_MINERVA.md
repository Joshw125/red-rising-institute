# House Minerva — The Owls

## Identity
**Theme:** Intelligence, strategy, tactical superiority
**Canon Reference:** House Minerva, the intellectual elite
**Starting Zone:** Highlands (Minerva Castle, Hex 6)
**Color:** Blue/Silver

---

## Starting Setup
- Starting Hexes: Minerva Castle (Hex 6) and surrounding Highlands
- Starting Resources: **7 Food** (v6.0)
- Units: 8 Regular, 1 Flag-bearer, 1 Tactician (Special) — 5 in supply for Recruit

---

## Player Board

### Passive Ability: Owl's Wisdom
*"She sees three moves ahead."*

**Minerva's scouting is enhanced. When Minerva has an Alert unit adjacent to an enemy hex, Minerva can see the exact unit count and readiness states in that hex (not just that enemies are present).**

Other Houses can see enemies in adjacent hexes but must guess at composition. Minerva knows exactly what's standing and what's laying down. This makes Minerva the master of picking fights they'll win.

---

### Special Unit: The Tactician
*"The Tactician doesn't fight. The Tactician makes sure you don't have to."*

**Can scout any hex within 2 hexes (not just adjacent) as a free ability — does not consume the Tactician's order.** The Tactician scouts AND does something else on the same turn.

The Tactician extends Minerva's vision far beyond what other Houses can see. Combined with Owl's Wisdom, the Tactician reveals exact compositions at range — ideal for identifying weak targets before committing to an attack.

---

### House Rule: Prepared Positions
*"The Owls are always ready. Even when they don't look it."*

**When Minerva is the defender, all At Ease Minerva units in that hex fight as Alert (+1 each instead of +0).**

This is Minerva's signature defensive ability. Deployment Strikes against Minerva are far less effective — their gatherers fight back. An attacker who sees 6 At Ease Minerva units expects 0 combat strength; they actually face 6. Combined with a Tactics Card, Minerva can turn an expected rout into a devastating counter.

**Attacking Minerva is dangerous. Attacking Minerva without scouting first is suicidal.**

*Note: This only applies when Minerva is defending (attacked by someone else). When Minerva initiates combat, At Ease units contribute 0 as normal.*

## Once Per Game: REMOVED (v6.0)
Strategic Genius was dropped in the anti-turtle pass. The teleport-3-units effect was strong but overlapped Minerva's "deliberate planning" identity awkwardly — Strategic Genius felt like raw board manipulation rather than tactical brilliance. May be reintroduced as a more focused effect.

---

## Resource Bonus (v6.0 — now mechanically enforced)
Minerva's Gatherers in Highland hexes produce **2 Food instead of 1** (prepared supply lines and efficient foraging). This is the only House resource bonus that survived the v6.0 pass — it's a clean economic edge that doesn't snowball, and it gives Minerva real reason to hold Highlands rather than abandon them.

---

## Strategic Notes

Minerva wins through **knowing more than everyone else**. Owl's Wisdom reveals exact enemy compositions. The Tactician scouts at range without spending an order. Prepared Positions punishes attackers who didn't do their homework.

**Early Game:** Establish Highland control. Highland Gatherers produce 2 Food each — Minerva starts with the strongest economic engine. Use the Tactician to scout for early threats.

**Mid Game:** Use superior information to pick profitable fights. Avoid even engagements — Minerva should only attack when they know they'll win. Prepared Positions makes ambushes against Minerva backfire spectacularly (At Ease Minerva fights as Alert on defense, so a Deployment Strike on Minerva gatherers turns into a fair fight).

**Late Game:** Minerva with a fortified castle (her Minerva Castle plus a captured neutral) is hard to dislodge. Combine Prepared Positions + +2 fortification = At Ease Minerva defends at full Alert strength under fortification. The defensive math is brutal for attackers.

**Minerva's Weakness:** Raw offensive combat power. Minerva has no combat strength bonuses on offense. Pursue capture VP carefully — castle assaults need 6+ strength concentrated, which means abandoning your defensive positions to send them. The Highland economy gives Minerva food breathing room to support such a push, but you have to commit.
