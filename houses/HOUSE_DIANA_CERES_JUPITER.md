# House Diana — The Shadows

## Identity
**Theme:** Speed, pursuit, striking anywhere
**Canon Reference:** House Diana, hunters of the Institute
**Starting Zone:** Greatwoods (Diana Castle, Hex 52)
**Color:** Silver/Green

---

## Starting Setup
- Starting Hexes: Diana Castle (Hex 52) and surrounding Greatwoods
- Starting Resources: **7 Food** (v6.0)
- Units: 8 Regular, 1 Flag-bearer, 1 Shadow (Special) — 5 in supply for Recruit

---

## Player Board

### Passive Ability: The Hunt (v6.0 — fires on any combat win)
*"Diana doesn't chase you. She's already there."*

**When Diana wins ANY combat (attack or defense), all surviving Diana units in that hex may immediately move 1 hex.** This movement is free (no order required) and does not change readiness.

This is Diana's signature ability. Win a fight, advance — or fall back. The Hunt rewards combat *participation*, not just initiation: a successful defense in Diana territory triggers a follow-up advance, an attack triggers pursuit. Chain attacks by hunting into the next hex, or counter-strike after weathering an assault.

**v6.0 change:** previously The Hunt only triggered on attacks Diana initiated. This conflicted with Diana's other strengths (defensive forest withdraws, ambushes via Shadow) — her most powerful ability never fired because her best strategies were defensive. Triggering on any win unifies her kit.

---

### Special Unit: The Shadow
*"She moves between the lines like smoke."*

**Can move through enemy-occupied hexes without triggering combat.** The Shadow passes through freely — enemies see nothing. The Shadow can also choose to stop in an enemy hex and initiate combat normally.

The Shadow is the ultimate scout. Send the Shadow behind enemy lines to gather intelligence, position for a rear attack, or slip past defenses to reach an objective.

---

### House Rule: Forest Hunters (v6.0 — replaces Phantom Stride)
*"They are gone before you knew they had moved."*

**When Diana withdraws units from a Greatwoods or North Woods hex to her Reserve Track, the Removal Marker is hidden for 1 round.** Opponents don't see the × marker on the map. Diana's deployment range is unknown until the marker is revealed at the start of the next round.

Combined with the universal forest-deployment-stealth rule (any house can deploy into forest hexes regardless of passive scouting), Diana effectively has a private forest tunnel system. Withdraw from forest → reappear from forest. Opponents don't know the origin or destination until it's too late.

**v6.0 change:** Phantom Stride (ignore terrain movement penalties) was dropped because terrain movement penalties aren't meaningfully enforced anywhere else in the game. Forest Hunters fits Diana's "Shadow" identity better — concealment + stealth + sudden appearances.

---

## Once Per Game: REMOVED (v6.0)
Lunar Eclipse was dropped along with the other once-per-game powers. Without a fully-implemented fog of war, Lunar Eclipse often did nothing (no scouting to disable). May be reintroduced once Owl's Wisdom and Tactician scouting are mechanically meaningful.

---

## Strategic Notes

Diana is the hunter. The Hunt turns every won attack into a pursuit. Phantom Stride means no terrain can slow Diana down. The Shadow scouts ahead. Lunar Eclipse blinds the prey before the killing blow.

**Early Game:** Use Greatwoods as a redeployment cradle. Withdraw forces to your Reserve Track via Forest Hunters (marker hidden for a round) and threaten anywhere. Diana benefits most from the universal "deploy into forest bypasses scouting" rule — opponents can't lock down forests with sentries.

**Mid Game:** The Hunt fires on any combat win. Defend in forest, win, advance into enemy territory — or attack and follow up. Pair with Shadow's pass-through-enemies movement for surgical strikes on Flag-bearers and gatherers.

**Late Game:** Diana's stealth advantage matters most when armies are thinned by Act III starvation. Sudden forest deployments behind enemy lines can swing flag captures or castle assaults.

**Diana's Weakness:** No combat strength bonus. Diana fights at standard strength — wins through positioning and surprise, not raw power. The forest combat cover that previously protected Diana's gatherers is gone (v6.0); now her gatherers need real defenders, and her edge comes from the redeployment loop.

# House Ceres (Expansion)

## Identity
**Theme:** Resources, sustainability, economic dominance
**Starting Zone:** North Woods (Hexes 4-5, 12-13, 21-23)
**Color:** Green/Brown

---

## Player Board

### Passive Ability: Bountiful Harvest
*"Where Ceres walks, the earth provides."*
**Once per round, double the Food production of one controlled Fertile hex (produces 2 Food instead of 1).**

### Special Unit: The Cultivator
*"She could grow wheat on bare stone."*
**Generates 1 Food in any hex it occupies (passive, per round), even in Barren hexes. The Cultivator makes any terrain productive.**

### House Rule: Fertile Ground
*"The forest feeds Ceres. The fields feed Ceres. Even the mountains feed Ceres."*
**Ceres units in Food-producing hexes cannot be subdued by Deployment Strikes.** They're so deeply rooted in the land that surprise attacks can't dislodge them — attackers must fight conventionally.

---

## Once Per Game: Golden Grain
**Gain 5 Food immediately. One-time emergency harvest.**

## Resource Bonus: +1 Food from Forest hexes.

# House Jupiter — The Bellona (Expansion)

## Identity
**Theme:** Defense, command, mountain dominance
**Starting Zone:** Mountains (Hexes 1-3, 9-11, 18-20)
**Color:** Purple/Gold

---

## Player Board

### Passive Ability: Fortress Mentality
*"The mountain is ours. Come and take it."*
**Controlled castle hexes produce +1 resource of any type (Jupiter's choice each round).**

### Special Unit: The Legionnaire
*"The Legionnaire doesn't break. Period."*
**Grants +1 combat strength to all defenders in the same hex. If subdued, automatically rallies at start of next round (no order required).**

### House Rule: Mountain Kings
*"Jupiter's warriors don't climb mountains. Mountains bow to Jupiter."*
**Jupiter units entering Mountain hexes do NOT spend their entire turn. They move into mountains normally (1 free move) and can still use their order.** This gives Jupiter unmatched mountain mobility — they can march through the mountains while other Houses crawl.

---

## Once Per Game: Thunderbolt Strike
**Automatically win one defensive combat in a hex you control.** No strength comparison — you win and may subdue/convert as if your margin of victory was 5 (3 subdues or 1 convert + 1 subdue).

## Resource Bonus: Castle hexes produce +1 resource of any type (per Passive Ability above).
