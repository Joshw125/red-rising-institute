# House Apollo — The Suns

## Identity
**Theme:** Range, mobility, precision strikes
**Canon Reference:** House Apollo, masters of reach and precision
**Starting Zone:** South Sea Coast (Apollo Castle, Hex 75)
**Color:** Gold/Yellow

---

## Starting Setup
- Starting Hexes: Apollo Castle (Hex 75) and surrounding South Sea Coast
- Starting Resources: **7 Food** (v6.0)
- Units: 8 Regular, 1 Flag-bearer, 1 Archer (Special) — 5 in supply for Recruit

---

## Player Board

### Passive Ability: Sun's Reach
*"Apollo's warriors cover ground like the sun crosses the sky."*

**Apollo's free move range is 2 hexes instead of 1.**

Every Apollo unit moves faster than every other House's units. On a standard turn, an Apollo unit can free-move 2 hexes + use Move Again as their order = 3 hexes of movement in one turn. This makes Apollo the most mobile House on the board and extends their effective deployment range from the Reserve Track by 1 hex (deploy, then free-move 2 instead of 1).

---

### Special Unit: The Archer
*"You don't see the Archer. You see the arrow."*

**Contributes +1 combat strength to any allied combat in an adjacent hex, without being in the fight.** The Archer stays in their hex — they're not at risk of being subdued or converted.

The Archer is a force multiplier. Position the Archer centrally and they support fights in any of 6 adjacent hexes. The Archer can also fight normally if enemies enter their hex (contributing +1 as a regular unit).

*Note: The Archer's adjacent support can only apply to ONE combat per turn.*

---

### House Rule: Healer's Art (v6.1 rework)
*"Apollo's warriors know the body as well as the blade."*

**After any combat Apollo is involved in, recall 1 Apollo unit from your Reserve Track to the combat hex (Alert, acted). Works whether Apollo won or lost.**

Healer's Art triggers automatically after every combat — no order required. Even if Apollo loses a fight (and the rest of his force retreats to RT), one Apollo unit gets called back to the combat hex immediately. This makes Apollo uniquely resilient in sustained combat: he always has a presence in the hex he just fought in.

(In v6.0, Healer's Art rallied subdued Apollo units in the combat hex — but v6.1 retired the persistent subdued state. The recall-from-RT version is the closest functional equivalent: Apollo's medics pull a wounded soldier back from the rear column to keep the line.)

---

## Once Per Game: REMOVED (v6.0)
Solar Supremacy was dropped in the anti-turtle pass. The "move through enemies" effect was situational and often went unused. Apollo's identity is already mobility-first via Sun's Reach (a permanent passive); a once-per-game version of the same theme was redundant.

---

## Resource Bonus
None in v6.0. (Previously: gather-and-move was Apollo's economic edge; this was dropped to keep economy uniform across Houses. Apollo's compensation is Sun's Reach, which lets Apollo Gatherers safely *swap hexes* with new gatherers via 2-hex free moves between rounds.)

---

## Strategic Notes

Apollo compensates for a slow start (isolated in the South Sea) with unmatched mobility and sustained combat resilience.

**Early Game:** Expand quickly using Sun's Reach (2-hex free move). Apollo covers more ground than any other House — claim territory and contest neutral castles before others can reach them.

**Mid Game:** Position the Archer centrally to support multiple fronts. Healer's Art keeps Apollo recovering after every fight. Aim for at least one castle capture (the only way Apollo banks Tactics Cards in v6.0, since combat wins no longer draw).

**Late Game:** Apollo's mobility means surprise flag captures and hex swaps. With 2-hex free moves, Apollo can reach Diana's flag through forest cover faster than Diana expects.

**Apollo's Weakness:** No defensive bonuses. Apollo has no terrain advantages, no prepared positions, no fortification bonuses. If caught flat-footed (At Ease, no Tactics Cards), Apollo folds like anyone else. Apollo's strength is choosing WHEN and WHERE to fight, not standing ground.
