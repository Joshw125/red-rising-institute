# Design Philosophy — Red Rising: The Institute

## Core Vision

Create a game that captures the **"beautiful chaos"** of the Institute competition — where no one knows who's winning until they suddenly lose, every turn has a discovery, and the lead changes constantly. Players should feel like Darrow: outnumbered, outmaneuvered, then somehow pulling off the impossible.

---

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Beautiful Chaos
- Multiple simultaneous victory paths prevent any single dominant strategy
- Constant lead changes through comeback mechanisms and event cards
- "Non-stop unexpected findings, good and bad" via Discovery Tokens
- No turn should feel routine

### 2. Meaningful Secrecy
- Hidden movement is *central*, not optional
- The Reserve Track creates tension without bookkeeping complexity
- Information has real value — scouting comes from keeping units Alert, not spending currency
- Opponents always have uncertainty about your plans

### 3. Thematic Authenticity
- **No killing** — only subduing and converting (the Institute's purpose is training, not murder)
- Strict Red Rising canon — no technology or elements from outside the books
- House identities match the series: Mars = aggression, Minerva = strategy, Diana = stealth, etc.
- Victory through leadership and conversion, not annihilation

---

## Design Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

1. **No elimination mechanics.** Units are subdued or converted, never removed from game.
2. **No non-canonical technology.** If it's not in Pierce Brown's Institute, it doesn't exist.
3. **Resources generate per hex, not per unit.** Per-unit generation creates unmanageable snowballing.
4. **Hidden movement requires no bookkeeping.** The Reserve Track solves this — never reintroduce tracking sheets.
5. **Multiple paths to victory.** Flag capture, domination, and VP all remain viable.

---

## The "Hope Is Never Lost" Principle

Every player needs a comeback mechanism:
- Mars: *Red Rising* — rally all subdued units once per game
- The event deck is weighted 40% comeback mechanics, 30% neutral, 30% leader pressure
- Discovery Tokens: 40% beneficial, 40% detrimental, 20% player choice
- Conversion means your worst defeat can become your enemy's weakness — every subdued unit is a potential convert

---

## Balance Philosophy

### Asymmetry Over Symmetry
Each House should feel like a different *game within the game*, not just different stats. Minerva players think differently than Mars players.

### Chaos That Rewards Skill
Events and discoveries introduce randomness, but skilled players:
- Anticipate event card probabilities
- Position for discovery site ambushes
- Use passive scouting (Alert units) to constrain Reserve Track deployment before committing

### Economic Balance Targets
- No player should ever have so many resources they stop caring about gathering
- No player should be so resource-starved they can't respond to threats
- Target: 3–5 meaningful resource decisions per turn

---

## What This Game Is NOT

- Not a wargame (no mass combat, no unit stacking bonuses)
- Not a euro game (resources serve aggression, not end-game VP formulas)
- Not a hidden traitor game (all Houses are openly competing)
- Not a deck-builder (pure action system — deck-building is a possible future variant only)

---

## Mechanical Inspirations

| Inspiration | What We Took |
|------------|-------------|
| *Arcs* (Leder Games) | Action system purity, asymmetric Houses |
| *Robinson Crusoe* | Discovery Token system (unexpected findings) |
| *Vast* | Deep House asymmetry, different win conditions |
| *Fury of Dracula* | Reserve Track hidden movement concept |
| *Root* | Faction identity driving strategic decisions |

---

## Future Expansion Notes

- **Olympus** is explicitly reserved for expansion content, not base game
- Deck-building variant is a future consideration (Josh noted its chaos potential)
- The Discovery Token and Equipment systems are designed to be modular for expansion
