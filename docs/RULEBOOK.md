# Rulebook — Red Rising: The Institute

> **2-4 players | 90-120 minutes | Medium-Heavy strategy**
> A game of hidden movement, deterministic combat, and fragile alliances on Mars.

---

## Overview

Players control asymmetric Houses competing in the Institute — a brutal training ground on Mars where young warriors fight for dominance. Win by capturing enemy flags, dominating the map, or accumulating Victory Points across 12 rounds divided into three escalating Acts.

**Core principles:**
- No unit is ever killed. Every soldier is subdued or converted. The Institute is a crucible, not a graveyard.
- Your army IS your action budget. More units = more you can do, but also more mouths to feed.
- Combat is deterministic. Count strength, compare totals. Drama comes from decisions, not dice.
- Tactics Cards are earned through exploration and conquest — castle captures, Discovery Sites, Proctor favor. Combat wins do **not** draw cards (anti-snowball).
- Information is power. Hidden movement, passive scouting, and forest concealment create constant tension.
- Aggression wins. The VP system rewards capturing castles, winning fights, and converting enemies — not sitting on territory.

## Setup

### Board Setup

1. Place the game board centrally.
2. Separate Proctor Cards into three decks by Act (Bronze I, Silver II, Gold III). Shuffle each separately. Place Act I deck face-down near the board.
3. Place Food tokens in a supply pool.
4. Place **5 Valor Tokens** on randomly selected hexes.
5. Shuffle all 20 **Discovery Tokens** face-down. Place 1 on each of the 20 designated locations (8 Discovery Sites, 3 Watchtowers, 3 Cavern Entrances, 6 hidden caches).
6. Shuffle the **12 Equipment Cards** face-down nearby. Shuffle the **Tactics Card** deck face-down nearby.
7. Place 1 face-down **Garrison Token** on each of the 6 neutral castles (Ceres, Jupiter, Juno, Mercury, Pluto, Neptune). Garrison strength is hidden from all players.
8. Determine first player randomly.

### Player Setup

1. Choose or randomly assign a House: **Mars, Minerva, Apollo, Diana**.
2. Take your House Player Board, all matching units, and control markers.
3. Collect starting Food: **7 Food**.
4. Take your units: **8 Regular + 1 Flag-bearer + 1 Special unit** (10 total to start; 5 remain in your supply for recruitment).
5. Deploy your 10 starting units to your House's starting hexes (see Player Board for placement).
6. Take your Reserve Track board and Removal Marker.
7. Draw 2 **Objective Cards**, choose 1 to keep (secret), return the other to the box.

*Note: No starting hand of cards. Tactics Cards are earned during play.*

## The Round

The game is played over **12 rounds** divided into three **Acts**:
- **Act I (Rounds 1-4): Survival** — Gentle starvation (1 Food per 5 units). Recruitment costs 3 Food. Reserve Track limited to Space 2.
- **Act II (Rounds 5-8): War** — Same starvation rate. Recruitment costs 3 Food. Reserve Track fully unlocked. Desperate Gambits available.
- **Act III (Rounds 9-12): The Reckoning** — Heavy starvation (1 Food per 3 units). Armies thin. Flag capture eased. Valor Tokens doubled.

### Each Round:

1. **Reveal Proctor Card** — Flip the top card of the current Act's Proctor deck. Read aloud. Apply its effect.
2. **Player Turns** — In clockwise order from first player, each player takes their full turn.
3. **End of Round** — Starvation, Food collection, Valor check, round advance.

First player rotates clockwise each round.

## Your Turn: Issue Orders

**There is no action limit.** On your turn, you issue orders to every unit you control. Each unit gets:

> **1 Free Move** (to an adjacent hex) **+ 1 Order**
>
> — OR —
>
> **Stay in place + 1 Order**

Your army is your action budget. More units = more things happening each turn, but more Food required to sustain them.

### Readiness (The Core Tension)

Units exist in one of two states, tracked by their physical orientation:

| State | Token | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Alert** | Standing upright | Combat-ready. Contributes +1 combat strength. Passively scouts adjacent hexes. |
| **At Ease** | Laying on side | Productive but vulnerable. Contributes +0 combat strength. No scouting. |

**The fundamental tradeoff:** You must Gather to eat. Gathering sets you At Ease. At Ease units contribute nothing in combat and can't see threats coming. Staying Alert means you're safe but starving. **The game lives in this tension.**

---

## Orders Reference

After a unit's free move (or choosing to stay), it executes **one** order:

### Movement & Positioning

| Order | Effect | Readiness |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Move Again** | Move 1 additional hex | Alert |
| **Hold** | Stay put, stand watch | Alert |

### Economy

| Order | Cost | Effect | Readiness |
|-------|------|--------|-----------|
| **Gather** | — | Collect 1 Food from this hex (Fertile or Castle). **Captured castles produce 2 Food.** Max 1 Gatherer per hex. | At Ease |
| **Recruit** | 3 Food | Add 1 new unit from your supply to this hex | At Ease |
| **Build Fortification** | **4 Food** | Place fortification marker (+2 combat strength for defender) | At Ease |

### Combat

| Order | Cost | Effect | Readiness |
|-------|------|--------|-----------|
| **Attack** | — | Initiate combat in this hex. Deterministic — count strength, compare, resolve. Both sides may play 1 Tactics Card. | Alert |

### Recovery

| Order | Cost | Effect | Readiness |
|-------|------|--------|-----------|
| **Rally** | — | Restore 1 subdued friendly unit in this hex to Active status | Alert |

### Reserve Track

| Order | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Withdraw** | Remove units from board → enter Reserve Track Space 1. Place Removal Marker on hex. |
| **Deploy** | Place units on board within range. Arrive **Alert**. Get free move + order this turn. |

*Reserve Track advancement (1→2→3) happens automatically at start of your turn — no order required.*

### Special

| Order | Cost | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Explore Discovery Site** | — | Draw 1 Equipment Card (first visit only, site then depleted) |

Alert units here see exact unit counts within 2 hexes (extended scouting).

## Combat

### The Deterministic System

Combat is fully deterministic. No dice. Count strength, compare totals, resolve.

### Step 1: Count Combat Strength

Each side totals:
- **+1 per Alert unit** (standing tokens)
- **+0 per At Ease unit** (laying tokens — not fighting)
- **Terrain modifiers** (Mountains +1 defense, Castle +2 if fortified). Forest gives **no** combat bonus — its protection is concealment, not cover (see Reserve Track).
- **Equipment modifiers** (Training Weights +1, etc.)
- **House bonuses** (Mars Red Rage +1 if outnumbered AND wins ties when outnumbered, Howler counts as 2, Minerva Prepared Positions makes At Ease Minerva fight as Alert when defending).

### Step 2: Tactics Cards (Simultaneous Reveal)

Both sides may play **1 Tactics Card** from their hand. Place face-down, reveal together. Neither side knows what the other will play.

Tactics Cards are the **only source of uncertainty** in combat.

### Step 3: Compare and Resolve

**Higher total wins. Ties go to defender** (Mars exception: when Mars attacks while outnumbered, Mars wins ties via Red Rage).

| Margin of Victory | "Blows" landed |
|-------------------|----------------|
| 1-2 | 1 |
| 3-4 | 2 |
| 5-6 | 3 |
| 7+ | 4 |

### Step 4: Aftermath (v6.1 — Losers retreat to Reserve Track)

Blows are applied to the loser's units in this priority order: **Alert non-flag → At Ease non-flag → special unit → flag-bearer last**.

For each blow, in priority order:
- **Conversion**: Up to `floor(blows / 2)` non-flag-bearer units are converted to the winner's house. Defected units stay in the hex, Alert, acted. Counts toward winner's 15-unit cap.
- **Flag-bearer hit**: A blow against the flag-bearer captures the flag (Subdued, stays in hex). In 2-player, this is an instant win.
- **All remaining loser units retreat** to the loser's Reserve Track Space 1, with the combat hex as their Removal Marker. They're normal RT units (no special wounded state) — they advance and deploy normally.

**Hex is the winner's** — uncontested. The loser's army isn't destroyed; it's hidden on the RT, ready to redeploy.

There is **no persistent "subdued in hex" state** in v6.1. Subdued status only applies to captured flag-bearers (so the flag-capture win condition still works). Combat is decisive: you win and take the hex, or you lose and your force regroups via the Reserve Track.

### Special Combat Rules

- **Flag-bearers:** Cannot be converted, only subdued
- **Forest terrain:** No combat bonus. Forest hexes instead protect via concealment — Reserve Track deployments INTO forest bypass passive scouting (see Reserve Track).
- **Neutral castle garrisons:** Hidden strength (2-4), fight as Alert defender with fortification (+2). Reduce strength to 0 to defeat the garrison.
- **Castle capture rewards (with Flag-bearer present when garrison falls):** garrison-strength worth of troops join you, draw 2 Tactics Cards, +5 VP one-time, castle pre-fortified for the new owner, and gather yields 2 Food while you hold it.
- **Deployment Strike:** Deploy from Reserve Track into all-At Ease hex = devastating ambush (your Alert vs their +0)

## Tactics Cards

**20 universal cards** earned through gameplay. The only source of combat uncertainty.

### How to Earn

| Source | Cards Earned |
|--------|-------------|
| Capture a neutral castle | Draw 2 |
| Search a Discovery Site (roll 5+) | Draw 1 (choose Equipment OR Tactics) |
| Certain Discovery Tokens | Varies (1-2 cards) |
| Certain Proctor Card effects | Varies |

> **Combat wins do NOT draw Tactics Cards.** Cards are earned through exploration and conquest, not raw combat — this prevents the bluff layer from snowballing to whoever struck first.

### In Combat

- Both sides may play **1 card**, revealed simultaneously
- Cards are **one-time use** — discard after playing
- Card effects range from +2/+3 strength to negating opponent's card to converting at 1:1

### The Decision

Every combat: *Do I play a card to guarantee this fight, or save it for a bigger moment?* Your opponent faces the same question. Neither knows what the other holds.

## The Reserve Track (Hidden March)

### How It Works

1. **Withdraw:** Remove units from a hex → place on Reserve Track Space 1. Place a Removal Marker on the origin hex (visible to all).
2. **Advance:** Each turn, units may advance 1 space (automatic, no order required).
3. **Deploy:** Place units on the board within range. Units arrive **Alert** and get their full turn (free move + order).

| Space | Range from Removal Marker |
|-------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Within 3 hexes |
| 2 | Within 6 hexes |
| 3 | Within 9 hexes |

### What Opponents See

- **Yes:** Units on track, unit count, which Space, Removal Marker location
- **No:** Destination, deployment timing

### Passive Scouting (Deployment Constraint)

**You cannot deploy from the Reserve Track within 1 hex of any enemy Alert unit — UNLESS the destination is a forest hex.**

Alert units are watching. Their 7-hex awareness zone (own hex + 6 adjacent) blocks surprise deployment. This means:
- At Ease targets in open terrain: Deploy directly on them (Deployment Strike)
- Alert targets: Deploy 2+ hexes away, march in with free move, attack with order
- **Forest hexes (Greatwoods / North Woods): always allow deployment** regardless of nearby Alert sentries. Forest concealment hides arrivals.

Alert units protect open terrain by keeping perimeter watch. But Alert units don't gather, and forests give them no help. **Forest mastery is its own strategic axis — Diana exploits this most, but any House can use forest hexes as a redeployment shortcut.**

### No Commitment

There is no secret notepad or destination card. Units on the track have no fixed destination until deployment. The player maintains full flexibility — the entire deployment radius is live.

### Key Rules

- Max **8 units** on Reserve Track at once
- Multiple groups allowed (each with own Removal Marker)
- Space 3 units **must deploy** this turn or next
- Reserve Track units **do not consume Food**
- Space 3 locked until Act II

## Resources & Economy

### Food (The Only Resource)

| Resource | Source | Primary Use |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| **Food** | Fertile hexes, Castles | Feeds army, recruits units, builds fortifications |

Castles produce **1 Food** when gathered.

### Gathering

- Assign a unit to Gather (1 order) → unit goes At Ease → collect Food at end of round
- **Max 1 Gatherer per hex**
- Only **Fertile hexes** and **Castles** produce Food. Barren hexes (Mountains, some Highlands) produce nothing.
- **Captured castles produce 2 Food** (was 1 before capture).
- **Minerva** in Highland Fertile hexes produces 2 Food (Highland Gatherers resource bonus).
- Contested hexes produce nothing

---

## Starvation

At the **end of each round** (before Food collection), pay Food.

| Act | Rate | 6 Units | 8 Units | 10 Units | 12 Units |
|-----|------|---------|---------|----------|----------|
| I | 1 Food per 5 units | 2 | 2 | 2 | 3 |
| II | 1 Food per 5 units | 2 | 2 | 2 | 3 |
| III | 1 Food per 3 units | 2 | 3 | 4 | 4 |

Round up. Reserve Track units do NOT require Food. Acts I & II share the same gentle rate so losing units mid-game stays recoverable; Act III is the squeeze.

### If You Can't Pay

For each unpaid Food, 1 active unit becomes **Weakened:**
- Cannot move, fight, gather, or act
- Can be rallied by spending **1 Food per unit** (automatic, no order)
- Not rallied by end of next round → deserts (returned to supply permanently)

---

## End of Round

In order:
1. **Starvation Upkeep** — Pay Food
2. **Food Collection** — Each Gatherer on a Fertile hex or Castle produces 1 Food
3. **Valor Token Check** — Players controlling Valor Token hexes choose: **2 VP or 2 Food** (4 VP in Act III)
4. **Valor Token Rotation** — Move all Valor Tokens to new random hexes
5. **Proctor Card Cleanup** — Discard current Proctor card
6. **Round Tracker** — Advance. If Round 4 or 8: Act Transition. If Round 12: final scoring.

---

## Act Transitions

### End of Round 4 → Act II
- Shuffle unused Act I Proctor cards into Act II deck
- **Reserve Track Space 3 now available**
- **Desperate Gambits now available** (1 per player per Act)
- (Recruitment cost is 3 Food in all Acts now — no Act II discount; the gentle Act I-II starvation already gives breathing room.)

### End of Round 8 → Act III
- Shuffle unused Act II cards into Act III deck
- **Starvation increases to 1 per 3 units** (was 1 per 5)
- **Flag-bearers can be captured from adjacent hexes** (not just same hex)
- **Valor Tokens worth 4 VP** (doubled from 2)
- Second Desperate Gambit available

## Victory Conditions

### Instant Win: Flag Capture

| Players | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| 2 | Capture 1 flag |
| 3 | Capture 2 of 2 enemy flags |
| 4 | Capture 2 of 3 enemy flags |

### Instant Win: Domination
Control **34+ hexes** (50%+) at end of any round.

### Victory Points (After Round 12)

| Source | VP |
|--------|-----|
| **Castle CAPTURE event** (one-time, when claimed) | **5 VP** |
| Neutral castle held at game end | 1 VP |
| Enemy home castle held at game end | 2 VP |
| Own home castle held | 0 VP |
| Each combat won (during game) | 1 VP |
| Each enemy unit converted (total) | 2 VP |
| Valor Token controlled at round end | 2 VP (4 VP in Act III) |
| Completed Objective Card | 2-5 VP |

The capture event pays 5 VP at the moment of claim and is irrevocable — even if the castle is retaken later, you keep the 5. End-of-game holding pays a small bonus on top, but turtling on castles is not a winning strategy by itself.

**Tiebreakers:** 1) Most castles. 2) Most combats won. 3) Most Food. 4) Most conversions.

## Desperate Gambits

Available from **Act II** onward. 1 per player per Act.

### 1. Scorched Earth
**Cost:** Destroy a hex you control.
**Effect:** Hex produces nothing for rest of game. All enemy units in adjacent hexes go At Ease.

### 2. Martyrdom
**Cost:** Permanently remove 1 unit from the game.
**Effect:** ALL subdued and Weakened units everywhere immediately recover. +1 combat strength this round.

### 3. False Flag
**Cost:** Strategic risk.
**Effect:** Swap your Flag-bearer's position with any regular unit. Both go face-down until revealed.

### 4. Last Stand
**Cost:** Units in target hex can never move again.
**Effect:** Permanently Alert, +2 combat strength, cannot be converted. Defensive auto-rally.

### 5. Scorpion's Tail
**Cost:** Must be used immediately after losing combat.
**Effect:** Retreat to adjacent hex (all units permanently Alert). Place hidden Trap marker — if enemy enters within 2 rounds, +3 combat strength for the defense.

## Houses (Quick Reference)

Each House has a **Player Board** with three abilities (always active) plus a Once Per Game power. No card decks.

### Mars — The Wolves
- **Passive (Red Rage):** +1 combat strength when outnumbered
- **Special Unit (Howler):** Counts as 2 Alert units in combat
- **House Rule (Blood Rally):** Win a combat → recall 1 Mars unit from your Reserve Track to the combat hex (Alert, acted)
- **Once Per Game (Rage of Ares):** Double combat strength for 1 battle

### Minerva — The Owls
- **Passive (Owl's Wisdom):** See exact counts + readiness in adjacent hexes
- **Special Unit (Tactician):** Scout within 2 hexes as free ability (no order consumed)
- **House Rule (Prepared Positions):** When defending, At Ease units fight as Alert
- **Once Per Game (Strategic Genius):** Teleport up to 3 units to any hexes

### Apollo — The Suns
- **Passive (Sun's Reach):** Free move range is 2 hexes instead of 1
- **Special Unit (Archer):** +1 strength to allied combat in adjacent hex (without being in the fight)
- **House Rule (Healer's Art):** After any combat Apollo is in (win or lose), recall 1 Apollo unit from your Reserve Track to the combat hex
- **Once Per Game (Solar Supremacy):** Move through enemy hexes without combat this turn

### Diana — The Shadows
- **Passive (The Hunt):** When Diana wins combat she initiated, all surviving attackers may move 1 hex immediately
- **Special Unit (Shadow):** Moves through enemy hexes without triggering combat
- **House Rule (Phantom Stride):** Diana's units ignore terrain movement penalties (Mountains cost normal movement)
- **Once Per Game (Lunar Eclipse):** All scouting fails globally for 1 round

## Diplomacy

There are **no formal alliance mechanics**. No tokens, no binding deals, no intent cards.

Diplomacy is entirely free-form: talk, whisper, bargain, betray. The game's hidden information systems create natural trust and distrust. This is the Institute — trust no one.

## Player Aid Summary

### Your Turn
1. For each unit: Free Move (optional) + 1 Order
2. Productive orders → At Ease | Military orders → Alert
3. Alert units passively scout adjacent hexes (block Reserve Track deployment)

### End of Round
1. Pay starvation (before collection!)
2. Collect from Gatherers
3. Valor check → VP or Food
4. Rotate Valor Tokens
5. Advance round tracker

### Key Numbers
- Starting Food: 7
- Recruit: 3 Food (any Act)
- Build Fortification: 4 Food
- Only resource: Food (from Fertile hexes and Castles)
- Max units: 15 (board + Reserve Track)
- Rally: 1 order = 1 unit restored (no die roll)
- Combat: Count Alert units + modifiers. Higher wins. Ties to defender (except Mars outnumbered → wins ties).
- Forest: no combat bonus, but Reserve Track deployments INTO forest bypass passive scouting.
- Castle capture: garrison-strength troops join you, 2 Tactics Cards, 5 VP one-time, pre-fortified, 2 Food/gather.
- Starvation: 1 Food per 5 units in Acts I-II; 1 Food per 3 in Act III.
