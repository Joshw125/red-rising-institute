# Tactics Cards — Earned Combat Modifiers

## Overview

Tactics Cards are the **only source of uncertainty** in combat. The base combat system is fully deterministic — count Alert units, add terrain, compare totals. Tactics Cards introduce controlled surprises that reward aggressive, exploratory play.

**Key Principles:**
- Cards are **earned through gameplay**, not dealt at start
- Every card in your hand represents a risk you took or a fight you won
- **One card per side, per combat**, revealed simultaneously
- Single use — played and discarded permanently
- Universal deck — not House-specific

---

## Acquiring Tactics Cards (v6.0 — anti-snowball)

**Combat wins do NOT draw cards.** This was changed in v6.0 (Decision 28) to prevent the bluff layer from snowballing to whoever struck first. Cards are now earned only through exploration and conquest:

### 1. Capture a Neutral Castle → Draw 2 Tactics Cards
Taking a garrison is hard (strength 2-4, +2 fortification). The payoff is large: troops, +5 VP, a fortified base, AND 2 Tactics Cards for future fights. Castle assaults are the single biggest card source.

### 2. Discovery Sites → Choose Equipment OR Tactics Card
When you successfully search a Discovery Site (roll 5+ with a unit present), choose: draw 1 Equipment Card OR draw 1 Tactics Card. Meaningful choice between permanent passive benefit and one-time combat surprise.

### 3. Certain Discovery Tokens
Some of the 20 hidden Discovery Tokens scattered across the map grant Tactics Cards:
- **Hidden Armory** (Token #2): Draw 1 Equipment Card AND 1 Tactics Card
- **Abandoned Siege Equipment** (Token #7): Draw 2 Tactics Cards
- **Supply Cache** (Token #1): Draw 1 Tactics Card + gain 2 Food

### 4. Proctor Card Rewards
Some Proctor cards distribute Tactics Cards — often to the trailing player (Favor of the Proctors), or to all players at once (Dawn March, Rumor Mill, Twilight Pact). Built-in catch-up mechanism.

**Why no combat-win draw?** Original design (v3.0-v5.0) gave the combat winner a card. In playtesting this concentrated cards in whoever attacked first, snowballing the bluff advantage. Removing combat draws makes the bluff layer about *exploration parity* rather than *aggression rewards*. Aggression is still strongly rewarded — through subdues, conversions, and capture VP — but the card economy is now distinct from the combat economy.

## Playing Tactics Cards in Combat

### Timing
After both sides count their base Combat Strength (Alert units + terrain + equipment + House bonuses), both sides simultaneously decide whether to play a Tactics Card.

### Simultaneous Reveal
Both players place their chosen card **face-down** (or announce "no card"). Reveal together. Apply both effects.

### One Card Per Side
Each side may play a maximum of **1 Tactics Card** per combat. Choose wisely.

### Discard After Use
Played Tactics Cards are permanently discarded. They do not return to the deck.

## Tactics Card List (20 Cards)

### Strength Cards (8 cards)
Raw combat power. The most common and broadly useful cards.

---

**1. Flanking Maneuver** (x2)
*"They came from the side. We never repositioned."*
**+2 combat strength.**
*The workhorse card. Good in any fight.*

---

**2. High Ground** (x2)
*"Elevation changes everything."*
**+2 combat strength. +3 if defending in Mountains or Highlands.**
*Defensive version of Flanking. Devastating in mountain fights.*

---

**3. Overwhelming Force** (x1)
*"There were just too many of them."*
**+3 combat strength.**
*The biggest raw strength card. Save it for the fight that matters.*

---

**4. Coordinated Assault** (x1)
*"Every unit hit at once. Perfect timing."*
**+1 combat strength per Alert unit you have in this combat (including base strength).**
*Scales with army size. A 5-unit force gets +5. Devastating with numbers.*

---

**5. Desperate Stand** (x2)
*"Nowhere to run. Might as well fight."*
**+1 combat strength for each unit you have FEWER than your opponent in this hex.**
*The comeback card. Being outnumbered 2 vs 5 gives +3. Pairs beautifully with Mars's Red Rage.*

---

### Tactical Cards (6 cards)
Abilities that change the rules of the fight.

---

**6. Shield Wall** (x2)
*"Lock shields. Hold the line."*
**Negate your opponent's Tactics Card.** Both cards are discarded, but neither takes effect.
*Pure denial. You don't know if they played a card, but if they did, it's gone.*

---

**7. Break Their Will** (x1)
*"They stopped fighting. Something in their eyes just... died."*
**This combat, convert at 1:1 instead of 2:1.**
*Terrifying with a large margin of victory. Win by 4 = convert 4 units instead of subduing 4.*

---

**8. Feigned Retreat** (x1)
*"They thought we were running."*
**If you lose this combat, move all your surviving units to an adjacent hex. No subdues are applied.**
*Insurance policy. Attack with confidence — if it goes wrong, you escape clean.*

---

**9. Snap to Attention** (x1)
*"On your feet. NOW."*
**All your At Ease units in this combat fight as Alert (+1 each instead of +0).**
*Transforms a vulnerable gathering force into a fighting force. Best when ambushed.*

---

**10. Caught Off Guard** (x1)
*"They looked ready. They weren't."*
**All opponent's Alert units in this combat fight as At Ease (+0 each instead of +1).**
*The inverse of Snap to Attention. Negates a prepared defense. Devastating against a small, elite Alert force.*

---

### Terrain Cards (3 cards)
Manipulate the battlefield itself.

---

**11. Scorched Earth** (x1)
*"If we can't hold it, no one can."*
**Negate all terrain modifiers for this combat.** Mountains, forests, fortifications — none of it matters.
*Best used when attacking a fortified castle (+2 negated) or forest hex (At Ease bonus negated).*

---

**12. Dig In** (x1)
*"They'd built positions overnight. Walls, stakes, firing lanes."*
**Gain the terrain modifier of a fortified castle (+2 defense) regardless of actual terrain.**
*Turn any hex into a fortress for one fight. Incredible on defense.*

---

**13. Forced March** (x1)
*"We covered the ground in half the time. They didn't expect us yet."*
**You may deploy or move 2 additional units from an adjacent hex into this combat before it resolves.** Those units arrive Alert.
*Reinforcements from next door. Changes the numbers mid-fight.*

---

### Special Cards (3 cards)
Unique effects that create memorable moments.

---

**14. The Reaper** (x1)
*"One warrior. One blade. The rest didn't matter."*
**Your Special Unit (if present) counts as 4 Alert units instead of its normal value.**
*Only works if your Special Unit is in the fight. Mars Howler = 4. Minerva Tactician = 4. Massive swing.*

---

**15. No Quarter** (x1)
*"Surrender was not offered."*
**All subdues from this combat become forced conversions (no 2:1 ratio required).** Conversions still respect the 15-unit cap.
*The nuclear option. Win big and absorb the entire enemy force.*

---

**16. Last Words** (x1)
*"They fought to the end. Every single one of them."*
**If you lose this combat, choose 1 enemy unit to subdue as you fall.** Applied after all other resolution.
*Even in defeat, you take someone with you. Can subdue a Special Unit or Flag-bearer.*

## Hand Management

### Hand Limit
No hand limit. You keep every Tactics Card you earn until you play it.

### Hidden Hand
Your Tactics Cards are hidden from opponents. They know you HAVE cards (they saw you win combats and capture castles), but not WHICH cards.

### The Decision
Every combat, you face the question: *Do I play a card to guarantee this fight, or save it for a bigger moment?* Your opponent is making the same calculation. Neither knows what the other is holding.

This is the Red Rising energy — not randomness, but **imperfect information and bold gambles.**

---

## Deck Management

### Shuffling
Shuffle all 20 Tactics Cards into a single deck at game start. Draw from the top when earning cards.

### Deck Exhaustion
When the deck runs out, no more Tactics Cards can be earned. Cards already in players' hands are still playable. This creates late-game scarcity — the last few cards are precious.

### No Trading
Tactics Cards cannot be traded between players. You earned them, they're yours.

---

## Interactions with Other Systems

### Equipment Cards
Equipment provides **permanent passive bonuses** (Training Weights = +1 in that hex). Tactics Cards provide **one-time surprises**. They complement each other — equipment is reliable, tactics are explosive.

### Discovery Sites
When searching a Discovery Site (roll 5+), the player CHOOSES: draw from the Equipment deck OR the Tactics deck. This choice is public — opponents see which deck you drew from.

### House Abilities
House passives and special units provide their bonuses **before** Tactics Cards are played. So Tactics Cards stack on top of House identity — Mars's Red Rage +1 AND a Flanking Maneuver +2 = massive swing.

### Proctor Cards
Some Proctor cards reference Tactics Cards:
- "Player with fewest VP draws 2 Tactics Cards"
- "All players discard 1 Tactics Card (if any)"
- "The next combat this round: both sides must play a Tactics Card if they have one"

## Designer Notes

### Why Universal Instead of House-Specific?
House-specific Tactics Cards would require designing, balancing, and tracking 80+ cards (20 per House). Universal cards are simpler: one 20-card deck, one set of rules. House identity comes from passives and special units, which are permanent. Tactics Cards are the universal wildcard layer on top.

### Why Earned Instead of Starting?
Starting with combat cards makes them feel like a default resource rather than a reward. Earning cards through combat, exploration, and castle capture means every card in your hand has a story — "I got this Overwhelming Force when I took Castle Ceres in Round 4." It also rewards aggressive play and punishes turtling: if you never fight or explore, you have no cards.

### Why Simultaneous Reveal?
Sequential card play (attacker plays first, defender reacts) gives too much information. Simultaneous reveal creates genuine uncertainty: you don't know if your opponent will play Shield Wall to negate your Overwhelming Force. The mind game is real.

### Why Only 20 Cards?
Scarcity creates value. With 20 cards and 4 players earning them throughout the game, each player might hold 3-5 at any time. Every card played is a real loss. This prevents "card spam" where players dump cards in every fight — you save them for the moments that matter.
