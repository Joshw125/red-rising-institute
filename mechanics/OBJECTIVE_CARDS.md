# Objective Cards

> **16 cards total.** Each player draws 2 at setup, keeps 1 (secret). The unchosen card is returned to the box.
> Scored at game end if the condition is met. All objectives are secret until revealed during final scoring.

---

## Design Principles

1. **Strategic divergence:** Each objective pushes the player toward a different strategy than default optimal play. Drawing your objective should change HOW you play, not just give you bonus VP for playing normally.
2. **Achievable but not guaranteed:** Every objective should be completable by any House, but require deliberate effort. ~60-70% completion rate in games where the player actively pursues it.
3. **Differential difficulty = differential VP:** Harder objectives pay more. Range is 2-5 VP.
4. **Trackable:** Conditions should be verifiable at game end (board state) or easily tracked with player board marks during play.
5. **No House-exclusive objectives:** Every objective is achievable by every House, though some naturally favor certain Houses. This creates interesting tension when Diana draws a mountain objective or Mars draws a territorial spread one.
6. **No overlap with instant wins:** No objective should say "capture a flag" or "control 34 hexes" — those are already game-ending conditions.

## Territory Objectives

### 1. The Conqueror
*"Four castles. Four banners. All mine."*

**Control 3+ non-home castles at game end.** (Neutral castles or captured enemy home castles all count.)

**VP: 4** | **Difficulty: Very Hard**

Requires holding castles across the map while 3 opponents try to take them back. Pairs well with aggressive expansion but demands the army strength to hold multiple positions simultaneously.

---

### 2. Forest Dominion
*"The trees are mine. Every last one."*

**Control 8+ Forest/Woods hexes at game end.** (North Woods and Greatwoods both count.)

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate**

Diana starts in the Greatwoods but doesn't own the North Woods. Any House can pursue this, but it requires territorial spread across the map's two forest regions. Forces conflict with Diana if you're not Diana, or expansion beyond the Greatwoods if you are.

---

### 3. Highland Sovereign
*"From the peaks, I see everything. And everything is mine."*

**Control 5+ Mountain or Highland hexes at game end.**

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate-Hard**

Mountains are expensive to enter (whole turn, arrive At Ease) but powerful to hold. This objective rewards the patience to climb and the strength to hold the heights. Jupiter-favored (Mountain Kings negates the movement penalty) but achievable by anyone willing to invest the tempo.

---

### 4. River Lord
*"Who controls the Argos controls the Institute."*

**Control 5+ Argos River hexes at game end.**

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate**

The river is central and contested. Its fast-travel mechanic makes it strategically valuable but exposed — river hexes are accessible from multiple directions. Holding 5 of the 8 river hexes requires sustained central dominance.

---

## Combat Objectives

### 5. War Machine
*"They stopped counting after the seventh."*

**Win 7+ combats over the course of the game.** (Tracked on player board — combat wins are already recorded for VP.)

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate-Hard**

You already earn 1 VP per combat win. This objective rewards consistent aggression with a bonus. 7 combats in 12 rounds means fighting more than every other round — a pace that demands careful Food management and tactical card economy.

---

### 6. The Raider
*"Asleep at their posts. As if I'd miss that."*

**Win 3+ combats where you attacked defenders who were all At Ease.** (Deployment Strikes count. Mark on player board when it happens.)

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate**

Rewards the game's core offensive mechanic. Finding and exploiting At Ease defenders requires good scouting, Reserve Track positioning, and timing. Each successful raid is devastating — this card rewards making it a habit.

---

### 7. Against the Odds
*"They had the numbers. I had the will."*

**Win 2+ combats where you had fewer total units than your opponent in the hex.** (Mark on player board when it happens.)

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate**

Mars thrives here (Red Rage gives +1 when outnumbered), but any House can do it with the right Tactics Cards, terrain modifiers, or readiness advantages. Rewards quality-over-quantity play and clever card usage.

---

### 8. Blood Price
*"I didn't come here to make friends. I came here to make an example."*

**Subdue or convert 10+ enemy units over the entire game.** (Track total on player board — including units you subdued even if they later rallied.)

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Hard**

Pure body count. 10 enemy units subdued/converted across 12 rounds is relentless. This isn't just "win fights" — it's "win fights decisively." High combat margins, targeting gatherer-heavy hexes, and sustained pressure are key.

---

## Tactical Objectives

### 9. Master Tactician
*"Every card played at the perfect moment. Luck had nothing to do with it."*

**Play 5+ Tactics Cards during the game.** (Track on player board. Tactics Cards that are discarded without effect — e.g., from Proctor Cards — don't count.)

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate**

Tactics Cards are earned through combat wins, castle captures, Discovery Sites, and Proctor cards. Playing 5 means you earned at least 5 AND chose to use them rather than hold them. Rewards engagement with the game's card economy.

---

### 10. Hidden March
*"They looked everywhere. Everywhere except where I was."*

**Win at least 1 combat using units that deployed from Reserve Track Space 3 (full 9-hex range) on the same turn they deployed.** (Mark when it happens.)

**VP: 4** | **Difficulty: Hard**

The ultimate Reserve Track play: withdraw, march all the way to Space 3, and deploy at maximum range for a devastating strike. Requires 3+ turns of commitment on the Reserve Track while the rest of your army holds the line. When it lands, it's a game-defining moment.

---

### 11. Discovery Hunter
*"I found things in the ruins they'd forgotten existed."*

**Claim 4+ Discovery Tokens during the game.** (Track on player board.)

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate**

8 Discovery Sites on the map, each with tokens to claim. Getting 4 means aggressively exploring while others fight. Some tokens grant Tactics Cards, equipment, or Food — the objective rewards the explorer playstyle that might otherwise lose tempo to combat-focused opponents.

---

### 12. Siege Master
*"Walls mean nothing when I'm the one knocking."*

**Capture 2+ neutral castles by defeating their garrisons.** (6 neutral castles exist: Ceres, Jupiter, Juno, Mercury, Pluto, Neptune.)

**VP: 4** | **Difficulty: Hard**

Neutral castle garrisons are hidden strength tokens (2-4 strength) that fight as Alert with +2 fortification. Defeating 2 garrisons means taking on two of the hardest fights on the map. Combined with the 4 VP per castle you already earn, this can be a dominant strategy — if you can hold them.

---

## Strategic Objectives

### 13. The Untouchable
*"My flag never touched the ground. Not once."*

**Your Flag-bearer is never subdued during the entire game.**

**VP: 4** | **Difficulty: Variable**

Easy if no one attacks you (but then you're probably losing). Hard if you're in the thick of combat. This objective creates an interesting tension: you want to fight (for VP) but must protect your Flag-bearer absolutely. Forces careful flag positioning, dedicated guards, or using the Reserve Track to keep your flag-bearer safe while your army fights.

---

### 14. Center of Power
*"Mercury Castle is mine. It has always been mine."*

**Control Mercury Castle at the end of 3+ different rounds** (need not be consecutive).

**VP: 4** | **Difficulty: Hard**

Mercury Castle sits on the central Argos River — the most contested position on the map. Holding it for 3 rounds requires sustained commitment to the center while fending off challengers from all directions. This is a statement objective: "I owned the middle of the board."

---

### 15. Barren Warden
*"They marched past the wastelands. I conquered them."*

**Control 8+ Barren hexes at game end.**

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate-Hard**

In a Food-only economy, Barren hexes produce nothing — most players avoid or pass through them. Holding 8 of the 26 Barren hexes on the map requires deliberate expansion into the Mountains, the rocky edges of the Highlands, and other unproductive terrain. The army occupying all that barren ground still needs to eat, creating real tension between territorial spread and starvation risk. Rewards players who turn the wastelands into a strategic fortress.

---

### 16. Watchtower Network
*"From the towers, my eyes see everything. Nothing moves without my knowledge."*

**Control 3+ Watchtower hexes at game end.**

**VP: 3** | **Difficulty: Moderate-Hard**

4 Watchtowers on the map provide enhanced scouting — Alert units on Watchtowers extend their passive scouting range, making Reserve Track deployment harder for opponents in a wider area. Controlling 3 of the 4 gives you information dominance across the board. Rewards spreading units to strategic positions rather than balling up in one area, and forces you to defend positions that don't directly produce Food.

**Expected contribution to winning score: ~3 VP (single objective)**

This sits within the VP_SYSTEM.md estimated range of 0-5 VP from objectives. A 3 VP objective is meaningful (~10-15% of a winning score) but never decisive alone.

## Physical Component Notes

- 16 cards, same card back (distinct from Tactics Cards and Proctor Cards)
- Card front: Name, flavor text, condition, VP value
- Compact text — condition should be readable in one sentence
- Players draw 2 at setup, keep 1, return the other facedown to the box
- Objectives are secret until final scoring

## Tracking Requirements

Some objectives require in-game tracking. The player board should include a small tracking area:

| Objective | Tracking Method |
|-----------|----------------|
| The Conqueror | Board state at game end — no tracking needed |
| Forest Dominion | Board state at game end |
| Highland Sovereign | Board state at game end |
| River Lord | Board state at game end |
| War Machine | Already tracked (combat wins) |
| The Raider | Mark on player board when Deployment Strike wins |
| Against the Odds | Mark on player board when outnumbered win |
| Blood Price | Tally on player board for each subdue/convert |
| Master Tactician | Tally on player board for each Tactics Card played |
| Hidden March | Mark on player board when Space 3 deploy + win occurs |
| Discovery Hunter | Tally on player board for each Discovery Token claimed |
| Siege Master | Mark on player board for each garrison defeated |
| The Untouchable | No tracking — verify at game end (Flag-bearer never subdued) |
| Center of Power | Tally rounds controlling Mercury Castle |
| Barren Warden | Board state at game end |
| Watchtower Network | Board state at game end |

**8 objectives** need no tracking (board state verification).
**8 objectives** need simple tally marks on the player board.
