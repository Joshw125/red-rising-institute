# Desperate Gambits

## Overview

Desperate Gambits are **powerful, irreversible actions** available from Act II onward. They cost something permanent — units, territory, or position — in exchange for a dramatic, game-altering effect.

Gambits capture the moments in *Red Rising* where someone does something shocking and irreversible: Darrow charging into impossible odds, the Jackal's scorched-earth cruelty, Mustang's strategic misdirection. These are the plays that make people talk about the game afterward.

**Availability:** Act II and Act III only (not Act I — the stakes aren't high enough yet).
**Limit:** Each player may use **1 Gambit per Act** (maximum 2 per game).
**Cost:** 1 order + the Gambit's specific permanent cost (Scorpion's Tail is the exception — it triggers in response to losing combat).

---

## The Gambits

### 1. Scorched Earth

*"If I can't have it, no one will."*

**Cost:** 1 order + destroy a hex you control
**Effect:**
- Remove all your units from the target hex (returned to your supply)
- Remove all resource markers, fortifications, and Discovery Tokens from the hex
- Place a **Scorched marker** on the hex — it produces no resources, cannot be fortified, and provides no terrain bonuses for the rest of the game
- All enemy units in **adjacent hexes** immediately go At Ease (the shockwave of destruction disrupts everyone nearby)

**Strategic Use:**
- Deny a critical hex to an advancing enemy (destroy a food hex they desperately need)
- Create a dead zone to protect your flank (no one will bother with a scorched hex)
- Trigger the At Ease effect to set up a follow-up attack on adjacent enemies
- Desperation defense — if you're about to lose territory, burn it so the winner gains nothing

**Thematic Fit:** The Jackal. Absolute ruthlessness. The willingness to destroy rather than surrender.

---

### 2. Martyrdom

*"Their sacrifice will not be forgotten. Rise."*

**Cost:** 1 order + permanently remove 1 of your units from the game (not to supply — gone forever, reduces your 15-unit maximum to 14)
**Effect:**
- **ALL** your subdued units everywhere on the board immediately rally (automatic)
- **ALL** your Weakened (starving) units immediately recover
- Your remaining units gain **+1 combat strength until end of round** (the inspiration of sacrifice)

**Strategic Use:**
- Comeback from a devastating combat where 4+ units were subdued
- Recovery from a starvation crisis (rally all Weakened units at once)
- The +1 combat strength bonus makes your next attack this round significantly stronger
- Best used when you have many subdued/weakened units (maximum value) and the permanent loss of 1 unit is worth the recovery of 4+

**Thematic Fit:** Darrow. Sacrifice yourself so others can fight. The leader who bleeds first and asks others to follow.

---

### 3. False Flag

*"The real target was never where you thought."*

**Cost:** 1 order (no permanent unit cost — the cost is strategic risk)
**Effect:**
- Secretly swap your **Flag-bearer's position** with any one of your regular units anywhere on the board
- This swap happens **in secret** — place both units face-down. Opponents don't know which unit is the Flag-bearer until combat reveals it or at end of game
- The swap is revealed when either unit is involved in combat or at final scoring
- If your Flag-bearer was about to be captured, this saves it — but now a different unit is vulnerable

**Strategic Use:**
- Move your Flag-bearer to safety when enemies are closing in
- Bait an enemy into attacking a "flag-bearer" position that's actually a regular unit (they waste an attack)
- Create uncertainty about where your flag is — opponents must commit to combat to find out
- Combine with Reserve Track: withdraw the "flag-bearer" (actually a regular unit), deploy it as a distraction, while your real flag is safe elsewhere

**Thematic Fit:** Mustang. Misdirection and contingency plans. Never be where they expect you.

---

### 4. Last Stand

*"They have the numbers. We have the ground. We have each other. That's enough."*

**Cost:** 1 order + your units in the target hex **cannot move for the rest of the game** (they are permanently rooted)
**Effect:**
- Choose a hex you control with at least 2 units
- Those units are **permanently Alert** (never go At Ease again)
- Those units gain **+2 combat strength** while defending in that hex
- Those units **cannot be converted** — only subdued (they will never join another House)
- If these units win a defensive combat, they immediately rally all subdued friendly units in the hex

**Strategic Use:**
- Lock down a critical hex (a castle, a food production center, a watchtower) with an unbreakable garrison
- Act III play when you need one position to hold at all costs
- Pairs with Jupiter's castle defense identity, but available to all Houses
- The permanent immobility is a real cost — those units can never help elsewhere

**Thematic Fit:** Pax. The loyal defender who stands when everyone else runs. The line in the sand that says "no further."

---

### 5. Scorpion's Tail

*"I let you win that battle. This was always my plan."*

**Cost:** Must be used immediately after losing a combat (no separate order required — this IS your response to defeat)
**Effect:**
- After losing a combat (any number of your units subdued/converted), your **remaining active units** may immediately:
  1. Retreat to any adjacent hex (free movement)
  2. All retreated units are **permanently Alert** until your next turn
  3. Place a **hidden Trap marker** on the retreat hex — if the enemy who beat you moves into that hex within 2 rounds, your units automatically get **+3 combat strength** for the defensive fight (the ambush bonus)
- The Trap marker is hidden — the enemy doesn't know you've set it
- The trap triggers once and is discarded. If no one enters, remove it after 2 rounds.

**Strategic Use:**
- Turn a defeat into a counter-ambush (lose the battle, win the war)
- Bait aggressive opponents into overextending (they win a fight, chase you, and walk into a prepared position)
- Particularly strong for Diana and Mars — Diana's Moonlight Veil in forests makes the trap invisible; Mars' Red Rage means even weakened forces fight hard

**Thematic Fit:** Darrow's tactical retreats. He loses battles to win wars. The feigned retreat that draws the enemy into a kill zone.

## Gambit Balance Notes

### Power Budget

Each Gambit should be worth approximately **4-6 actions** of normal play. They're game-altering but not game-winning on their own — they create opportunities that still require good play to capitalize on.

### Counterplay

Every Gambit should have counterplay:
- **Scorched Earth:** The user loses a hex permanently. If they scorched a hex they needed, they hurt themselves.
- **Martyrdom:** Costs a permanent unit. If you only had 1 subdued unit, you paid 1 to recover 1 — terrible trade.
- **False Flag:** Only revealed by combat — but Minerva's Owl's Wisdom can see readiness states in adjacent hexes, which may help identify which unit is the Flag-bearer based on behavior. Committing to an attack is the only way to be sure.
- **Last Stand:** Immobile units can be starved out (cut food supply to that hex) or simply bypassed. They'll never gather again, so they drain Food without producing.
- **Scorpion's Tail:** Only triggers if the enemy pursues. A patient enemy can simply go around or wait 2 rounds for the trap to expire.

### Playtesting Watch Items

- [ ] Are Gambits used in > 80% of games? (Too necessary = baseline power is too weak)
- [ ] Are Gambits used in < 30% of games? (Too costly = not worth the sacrifice)
- [ ] Is one Gambit chosen > 50% of the time? (Dominant Gambit = rebalance costs/effects)
- [ ] Do Gambits create memorable moments? (The whole point — if they're boring, redesign)
- [ ] Is the 1-per-Act limit right? (If players want to use 2 in Act III, consider allowing it at increased cost)
