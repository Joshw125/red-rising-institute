# Equipment Cards

## Overview

Equipment Cards represent physical items discoverable at Institute facilities. They are found by revealing units at **Discovery Sites** on the map and searching — a deliberate, risky action that exposes your forces to ambush.

**12 Equipment Cards total** — all book-accurate to Red Rising canon.

---

## Acquiring Equipment

All Equipment Cards come from a single **12-card deck**. When the deck is empty, no more equipment can be acquired by any method. Equipment is finite and contested — early exploration matters.

### Method 1: Explore a Discovery Site

**Cost:** 1 order
**Requirements:** At least 1 unit at an unexplored Discovery Site hex
**Process:** Draw 1 Equipment Card from the deck. **Guaranteed — no dice roll.** Mark the site as explored (it cannot be searched again).

**Risk:** Reaching a Discovery Site means extending your forces into contested territory. Opponents can see your exposed units and may attack.

There are **8 Discovery Sites** on the map. First player to arrive and search claims the equipment. This is the primary way equipment enters the game — free but competitive.

### Method 2: Discovery Token (Hidden Armory)

Some Discovery Tokens (e.g., Hidden Armory) grant Equipment Cards when triggered. These also draw from the shared 12-card deck.

## Equipment Card List (12 cards)

All items are canonical to the Red Rising universe / Institute setting.

---

**1. GravBoots**
*"The boots hum with magnetic field coils. Each step is lighter than the last."*

**Effect:** +1 hex to your deployment range for each Reserve Track space.
*(Space 1 = 4 hexes instead of 3; Space 2 = 7 hexes; Space 3 = 10 hexes)*

**Hold:** Permanent until used. Attach to a specific unit group.
**Limit:** 1 per player.

---

**2. Medical Kit**
*"Institute-standard trauma pack. Stimulants, bio-foam, nerve blocks."*

**Effect:** When you Rally a subdued unit, rally a second subdued unit in the same hex for free (1 order restores 2 units instead of 1). Permanent.

**Hold:** Permanent passive benefit.
**Limit:** 1 per player. If you already have one, discard the duplicate and gain 2 Food.

---

**3. Climbing Gear**
*"Pitons, rope, harness. Mountain-rated."*

**Effect:** Mountains no longer cost a unit's entire turn to enter. Units with Climbing Gear move into mountains normally (1 free move) and can still use their order.

**Hold:** Permanent passive benefit.

---

**4. Emergency Rations**
*"Freeze-dried, vacuum-sealed. Enough for a cohort."*

**Effect:** Gain 3 Food immediately when discovered. Then discard.

**Hold:** One-time use (consumed on discovery).

---

**5. Institute Maps**
*"Survey data. Someone marked the patrol routes in red."*

**Effect:** Reveal the exact unit count and readiness states of all enemy units in any 3 hexes of your choice (ignoring Moonlight Veil). Then discard.

**Hold:** Can be saved for later use. Single use.

---

**6. Signal Device**
*"A small transmitter. Encrypted channels, tight beam."*

**Effect (Passive):** Your Alert units' passive scouting range extends to **2 hexes** instead of 1. Reserve Track deployment is blocked within 2 hexes of your Alert units holding a Signal Device.

**Hold:** Permanent passive benefit. Attach to a specific unit or hex.

---

**7. Survival Gear**
*"Rated for -50C. Radiation shielding. Rebreather included."*

**Effect (Passive):** Your units in this hex are immune to negative Proctor Card effects.

**Hold:** Attached to a specific hex (not a unit). Permanent.
*(If you abandon the hex, the gear stays — can be found by opponents.)*

---

**8. Training Weights**
*"Resistance bands, weighted vests. The kind that makes you faster by making everything harder."*

**Effect:** Units in the same hex as this card gain **+1 combat strength** (stacks with Alert status).

**Hold:** Attached to a hex. Permanent while held.
*(Must have an active unit in the hex to maintain benefit.)*

---

**9. Institute Tools**
*"Multi-function kit. The engineers left one behind."*

**Effect (One-time):** Change 1 Barren hex to Fertile for the rest of the game (it now produces 1 Food when gathered).

**Hold:** One-time use, then discard.

---

**10. Advanced Bandages**
*"Bioweave, self-adhesive, stimulant-infused. Subdued units recover."*

**Effect (Passive):** Subdued units in the same hex as this card automatically rally at the start of each round (no order required).

**Hold:** Attached to a hex. Permanent.
*(Moves with you if you pick it up — counts as carrying it, 1 unit must remain active in that hex.)*

---

**11. Navigation Equipment**
*"Topographic overlays, landmark triangulation, magnetic compass."*

**Effect:** Your units may move along river hexes **unlimited distance** for 1 action (instead of normal river movement rules).

**Hold:** Permanent passive benefit for all your units.

---

**12. Hidden Cache**
*"Someone buried this before the Institute began. Centuries old, still sealed."*

**Effect:** Gain **4 Food** immediately when discovered. Then discard.

**Hold:** One-time use (consumed on discovery).

---

## Equipment Rules

### Holding Equipment
- Equipment Cards are held in front of you, visible to all players
- No hand limit for equipment
- Multiple equipment cards can be held simultaneously
- Equipment is NOT lost when units are subdued (it's yours until used)

### Transferring Equipment
- You cannot trade Equipment Cards to other players

### Discarding Equipment
- One-time use cards automatically discard after use
- Permanent cards can be voluntarily discarded (1 order) to deny opponents from capturing them

### Scarcity
With only 12 cards in the deck and 8 Discovery Sites plus Discovery Tokens all drawing from the same pool, equipment is a **contested finite resource**. In a 4-player game, average ~3 cards per player if evenly distributed. Early explorers grab the best stuff. When the deck runs out, it's gone.

### Ambush Risk
Reaching a Discovery Site means extending into contested territory. Opponents can attack your exposed units. Equipment is powerful, but claiming it costs position and tempo.

---

## Equipment Design Notes

All 12 items were designed to be recognizable to Red Rising readers while serving distinct mechanical niches:
- **Mobility:** GravBoots, Climbing Gear, Navigation Equipment
- **Food/Survival:** Emergency Rations, Hidden Cache, Medical Kit
- **Information:** Institute Maps, Signal Device
- **Combat:** Training Weights, Advanced Bandages
- **Utility:** Survival Gear, Institute Tools

No equipment is strictly better than another — situational advantage varies by House and game state.
