# Discovery Token System

## Overview

Discovery Tokens are Robinson Crusoe-style unexpected findings placed at specific locations on the map. The goal is "non-stop unexpected findings, good and bad" — every exploration should feel like it might uncover something that changes the game.

**Balance Target:** 40% beneficial / 40% detrimental / 20% player choice

---

## Triggering Discovery

When a player reveals units in a hex containing a Discovery Token:
1. Immediately flip the token face-up
2. Read the effect aloud
3. Apply the effect (or make the choice if it's a choice token)
4. Remove the token from the map (single use)

A player can move through a hex without triggering discovery — only units that stop and interact (Gather, Hold, etc.) trigger it.

---

## Token Placement

**20 Discovery Tokens total** distributed across 6 designated Discovery Sites plus additional hidden caches scattered across the map:
- Discovery Sites (6 locations — see [MAP_OVERVIEW.md](../map/MAP_OVERVIEW.md))
- Subterranean Cavern entrances (3 locations)
- Watchtower Peaks (3 locations)
- Hidden Caches scattered across terrain (8 locations)

Placement is randomized each game from the face-down token pool.

## Token List (20 Tokens)

### Beneficial Tokens (8 tokens — 40%)

**1. Supply Cache**
*"Darrow stumbled upon weapons and rations left by an earlier cohort."*
Gain 2 Food immediately and draw 1 Tactics Card.

**2. Hidden Armory**
*"Behind the waterfall, rows of equipment, untouched."*
Draw 1 Equipment Card AND 1 Tactics Card.

**3. Loyal Remnant**
*"A small band, forgotten by their House, pledges to your cause."*
Add 2 Regular units to this hex immediately.

**4. Scout's Report**
*"A lookout left detailed notes. Every House position is marked."*
Look at 1 opponent's Objective Card AND see exact unit counts and readiness states in any 3 hexes of your choice.

**5. Favorable Terrain**
*"This ridge provides perfect sight lines. You see everything."*
For the rest of this round, you may see exact unit counts and readiness states in ALL hexes on the map.

**6. Subterranean Passage**
*"A tunnel system, hand-carved. It goes on for kilometers."*
This hex gains the Subterranean Cavern special ability for the rest of the game (permanent). Your units may use it immediately.

**7. Abandoned Siege Equipment**
*"Iron and ancient tech, still functional."*
Draw 2 Tactics Cards.

**8. Medic's Cache**
*"Bandages, stimulants, field surgery equipment."*
Immediately rally all your subdued units in all hexes. No order required.

---

### Detrimental Tokens (8 tokens — 40%)

**9. Ambush!**
*"They were waiting for you."*
An opponent of your choice (or the player with most VP if all decline) may immediately move up to 3 of their units to this hex and initiate combat. They move for free.

**10. Trap**
*"The floor gives way."*
1 of your units in this hex is immediately subdued (your choice which). If you have a Special Unit here, it must be the one subdued.

**11. Diseased Water**
*"The spring looked clean."*
Lose 3 Food. If you have fewer than 3 Food, lose all Food.

**12. False Maps**
*"Someone wanted you to waste time here."*
This unit's order is wasted — it cannot perform any order this turn.

**13. Unstable Ground**
*"The earth shudders."*
All your units in this hex must immediately move to an adjacent hex of your choice. If no adjacent hex is available, they are subdued.

**14. Rival Scouts**
*"You weren't the only ones looking for this."*
The player with the fewest units immediately gains 2 Food and may see exact unit compositions of all hexes within 3 of this location. (If tied, all tied players gain the benefit.)

**15. Equipment Failure**
*"The cold does things to machinery."*
If you have any Equipment Cards, discard one of your choice. If none, lose 2 Food.

**16. Signal Flare**
*"They know exactly where you are now."*
Your position in this hex is publicly revealed. For the next 2 rounds, all opponents know your exact unit count and readiness here automatically.

---

### Choice Tokens (4 tokens — 20%)

**17. The Prisoner**
*"A soldier, bound and gagged. They look at you with fear, then something else."*
**Choose one:**
A) Convert them — Add 1 Regular unit to your forces immediately.
B) Interrogate them — Look at all opponents' Objective Cards. (They talk in exchange for freedom.)
C) Leave them — Nothing happens, but remove this token.

**18. The Hidden Bunker**
*"It's stocked. Enough for a House. But it's buried deep."*
**Choose one:**
A) Fortify — This hex gains a permanent fortification (+2 defense) with no Food cost.
B) Strip it — Gain 6 Food immediately, but the hex is permanently exhausted (produces no Food for rest of game).

**19. The Codes**
*"Encrypted maps showing food caches and enemy positions."*
**Choose one:**
A) Keep secret — Gain 3 Food and look at all opponents' Objective Cards secretly.
B) Broadcast — All players gain 1 Food, but you gain 2 VP.

**20. The Deserter**
*"She wears Minerva's colors but she's done with them. She has skills."*
**Choose one:**
A) Recruit — Pay 2 Food. Gain 1 additional unit that counts as a Special Unit for Tactics Card purposes (The Reaper card applies to it).
B) Interrogate — Free. Gain information: that player must reveal the exact count and positions of all their Reserve Track groups.
C) Send back — Gain 1 VP and draw 1 Tactics Card.

---

## Interaction with Equipment Cards

Some Discovery Tokens grant Equipment Cards (see token #2: Hidden Armory). Equipment Cards are a separate card deck — not all discoveries lead to equipment, but the Hidden Armory token is the primary non-Discovery Site way to acquire them.

See [EQUIPMENT_CARDS.md](EQUIPMENT_CARDS.md) for the full Equipment Card list.

---

## Replayability Note

Discovery Tokens are shuffled and placed randomly each game. Combined with variable Proctor Cards and Objective Cards, this means no two games have the same discovery pattern. A cave that held an Ambush last game might hold a Supply Cache this game — players can't rely on memorized positions.
