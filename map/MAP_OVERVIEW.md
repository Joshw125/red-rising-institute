# Map Overview — The Institute on Mars

## Food-Only Economy

The Institute runs on a single resource: **Food**. Hexes are either **Fertile** (produces 1 Food when gathered) or **Barren** (no production, but may have tactical terrain value). Castles also produce 1 Food when gathered.

There is no Tech, no Intel, no secondary currency. Food feeds your army, recruits new units, and builds fortifications. Every decision is a food decision.

---

## Terrain Regions

The Institute map covers 90 hexes across 8 terrain regions in a diamond layout. Each region has distinct terrain properties and food density.

---

### Mountains (Northwestern) — 14 hexes
**Food Density:** Very Low (2 fertile, 11 barren)
**Movement:** Entering a mountain hex costs a unit's entire turn (free move consumed, no order). Arrives At Ease.
**Combat Bonus:** +1 defense for defender (high ground)
**Notes:** Barren peaks and rocky ridges. Only 2 fertile hexes (a mountain spring and a foothills meadow). Strategically powerful for defense but economically dead — holding mountains drains your food supply. Contains Jupiter Castle (neutral), Mountain Watch watchtower, 1 Cavern entrance, 1 Discovery Site.

---

### North Woods (Northern) — 10 hexes
**Food Density:** Very High (9 fertile, 0 barren)
**Movement:** Normal (1 free move).
**Combat Bonus:** At Ease units fight at +1 (forest cover)
**Notes:** The breadbasket. Almost every hex is fertile — dense forest with abundant game, edible plants, and fresh water. Contains Ceres Castle (neutral), 2 Discovery Sites. Borders Mountains and Highlands — a natural flashpoint between Mars and Minerva. The most food-dense non-castle region on the map.

---

### Highlands (Northeastern) — 10 hexes
**Food Density:** Low (3 fertile, 6 barren)
**Movement:** Normal (1 free move)
**Notes:** Minerva's home territory. Exposed ridges and windswept plateaus with scattered fertile valleys. Minerva must expand toward the North Woods or Postern Hills to feed a growing army. Contains Minerva Castle (home), Highland Watch watchtower, 1 Discovery Site. Borders Postern Hills (east) and Greatwoods (south).

---

### Postern Hills (Far East) — 6 hexes
**Food Density:** Moderate (3 fertile, 2 barren)
**Movement:** Normal (1 free move)
**Notes:** Rolling hills east of the Highlands. Enough food to sustain a small force. Contains Juno Castle (neutral) — contested between Minerva and Diana. Small enough to control quickly but exposed on multiple sides.

---

### Argos River (Central Corridor) — 10 hexes
**Food Density:** Very High (8 fertile, 1 barren)
**Movement:** Fast travel — move between any two connected river hexes as the free move
**Notes:** The most valuable contested territory. River valley is fertile (fishing, irrigation, foraging), AND the river provides fast travel. Mercury Castle (neutral) sits at the central crossing — the highest-value target on the map. River Watch watchtower overlooks approaches. Exposed terrain (no combat bonuses) but strategically vital. Contains 1 Discovery Site.

---

### Greatwoods (Eastern) — 18 hexes
**Food Density:** High (14 fertile, 3 barren)
**Movement:** Normal.
**Combat Bonus:** At Ease units fight at +1 (forest cover, same as North Woods)
**Notes:** The largest region. Diana's home territory. Abundant game and foraging in dense forest, with a few rocky clearings and thickets that produce nothing. Contains Diana Castle (home), Greatwoods Cavern entrance, 1 Discovery Site.

---

### Southern Reaches / Foothills (Southwestern) — 12 hexes
**Food Density:** Moderate-High (8 fertile, 2 barren)
**Movement:** Normal
**Notes:** Rocky transitional terrain between mountains and coast. Some fertile valleys, some barren passes. Mars Castle (home) and Pluto Castle (neutral, near cavern entrance) both here. Contains 1 Cavern entrance, 1 Discovery Site.

---

### South Sea Coast (Southern) — 10 hexes
**Food Density:** High (7 fertile, 1 barren)
**Movement:** Normal
**Notes:** Apollo's home territory. Fishing villages, tidal pools, and coastal foraging provide reliable food. One rocky headland produces nothing. Contains Apollo Castle (home), Neptune Castle (neutral, contested with Diana), Coastal Watch watchtower, 1 Discovery Site.

---

## Castles

### Home Castles (4) — Player Starting Positions

| Castle | House | Hex | Location | Terrain |
|--------|-------|-----|----------|---------|
| **Minerva Castle** | Minerva | 7 | NE highlands | Elevated, open |
| **Mars Castle** | Mars | 52 | W foothills | Rocky, elevated |
| **Diana Castle** | Diana | 59 | E Greatwoods | Dense forest |
| **Apollo Castle** | Apollo | 88 | S coast | Open, flat |

- Pre-fortified (+2 defense)
- Worth **0 VP** (you don't score for what you started with)
- **Enemy home castles are worth 4 VP** if captured
- Produce **1 Food** when a Gatherer is assigned

### Neutral Castles (6) — Objectives with Hidden Garrisons

| Castle | Hex | Location | Terrain | Nearest Players |
|--------|-----|----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Jupiter Castle** | 10 | NW Mountains | Mountain | Mars (~4 hexes) |
| **Ceres Castle** | 22 | North Woods | Forest | Mars, Minerva (~3-4 hexes) |
| **Juno Castle** | 37 | Postern Hills | Hills | Minerva (~4 hexes), Diana (~4 hexes) |
| **Mercury Castle** | 44 | Central river | Riverside | Everyone (~4 hexes) |
| **Pluto Castle** | 74 | SW, near caverns | Rocky | Mars (~3 hexes) |
| **Neptune Castle** | 81 | SE coast | Coastal | Apollo (~3 hexes), Diana (~4 hexes) |

- **Hidden garrison token** (strength 2, 3, or 4) placed face-down at setup
- To scout: Alert unit adjacent to castle can peek at garrison strength (free)
- To claim: defeat the garrison in combat **with your Flag-bearer present** (garrison fights as Alert defender with +2 fortification)
- **Capture rewards:** Surrendered troops (1-2 units) + 1 Tactics Card
- Once claimed: pre-fortified (+2 defense), produces 1 Food when gathered
- **Worth 4 VP at game end**

### Castle VP Summary

| Scenario | VP |
|----------|-----|
| Holding a neutral castle at game end | 4 VP |
| Capturing an enemy home castle | 4 VP |
| Holding your own home castle | 0 VP |

## Special Hexes

### Watchtowers (4)

| Watchtower | Hex | Location | Overlooks |
|------------|-----|----------|-----------|
| Mountain Watch | 20 | NW mountains | North Woods, mountain passes |
| Highland Watch | 26 | NE highlands | Greatwoods edge, eastern approaches |
| River Watch | 55 | Central river | River corridor, Mercury Castle approaches |
| Coastal Watch | 90 | S coast | Southern coast, Neptune Castle area |

- Alert units here see exact unit counts and readiness states within **2 hexes** (extended scouting)
- Block Reserve Track deployment within **2 hexes** instead of 1

### Cavern Network (3 entrances)

| Cavern | Hex | Location | Connected To |
|--------|-----|----------|-------------|
| Mountain Cavern | 11 | NW mountains (near Jupiter Castle) | Pluto Cavern, Greatwoods Cavern |
| Pluto Cavern | 75 | SW (near Pluto Castle) | Mountain Cavern, Greatwoods Cavern |
| Greatwoods Cavern | 73 | E Greatwoods (Diana's territory) | Mountain Cavern, Pluto Cavern |

- A unit at a cavern entrance can move to any other cavern entrance as its free move
- Units in caverns are hidden from scouting
- The shortcut system — enables surprise flanking routes across the map

### Discovery Sites (8)

First player to explore claims 1 Equipment Card (deterministic, site is then depleted).

- 2 in North Woods (hexes 13, 31)
- 1 in Mountains (hex 29, mountain spring)
- 1 in Highlands (hex 25, highland plateau)
- 1 in Argos River (hex 43, river crossing)
- 1 in Greatwoods (hex 71, deep forest)
- 1 in Southern Reaches (hex 83, SW frontier)
- 1 in South Sea Coast (hex 87, coastal site)

## Strategic Map Analysis

### High Value Targets

| Priority | Target | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Mercury Castle (center, hex 44) | Central position, river access + fast travel, everyone contests it |
| 2 | Ceres Castle (North Woods, hex 22) | Food-rich region, Mars/Minerva flashpoint |
| 3 | Argos River hexes | 8 fertile hexes + fast travel = the map's best corridor |
| 4 | North Woods | 9 fertile hexes in 10 — the densest food region |
| 5 | Neptune Castle (SE coast, hex 81) | Apollo/Diana flashpoint, food-rich area |
| 6 | Juno Castle (Postern Hills, hex 37) | Minerva/Diana contested |
| 7 | Watchtowers | Extended scouting, deployment denial |
| 8 | Cavern entrances | Surprise movement network |

### Barren Zones

Mountains are an economic dead zone — 11 of 14 hexes produce nothing. Holding mountains costs food without producing any. This makes them purely defensive terrain: excellent for blocking attackers (difficult terrain + high ground bonus) but expensive to garrison. Jupiter Castle is the exception — a food-producing fortress surrounded by barren rock.

### Choke Points

- **Argos River corridor** — whoever controls the central river hexes controls food supply AND north-south movement
- **North Woods / Highlands border** — where Mars and Minerva first clash over Ceres Castle and the breadbasket
- **Postern Hills** — small region, easy to lock down, but exposed from multiple directions
- **Mountain passes (hexes 29, 40)** — the only fertile mountain hexes, gateways between mountains and lowlands
- **Cavern exits** — units emerging from caverns can appear behind enemy lines

### Natural Rivalries

| Rivalry | Over What | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Mars vs. Minerva | Ceres Castle + North Woods food | Both need food, both ~3-4 hexes away |
| Minerva vs. Diana | Juno Castle + Postern Hills food | Minerva is food-poor, Diana can project from Greatwoods |
| Apollo vs. Diana | Neptune Castle + SE food | Both nearby, both food-hungry in late game |
| Diana vs. Mars | Pluto Castle / cavern network | Diana can reach Pluto through Greatwoods Cavern |
| Apollo vs. Everyone | Mercury Castle | Apollo has river speed; Mercury is the biggest prize |

## Valor Token Rotation

3-5 Valor Tokens placed on random hexes at the start of each round. Controlling them at round end grants:
- 2 VP, OR
- 2 Food

Tokens rotate each round — no hex is permanently a Valor Token location. This drives conflict across the whole map.
